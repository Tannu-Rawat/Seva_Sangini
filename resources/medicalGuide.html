Basic Assessment Guidelines

  • Check vital signs: Measure temperature, pulse, respiratory rate, and blood pressure if possible.
  • Screen for danger signs: Look for severe pain, breathlessness, confusion, inability to eat/drink, or persistent vomiting.
  • Assess for infection: Ask about fever, cough, wounds, or urinary symptoms.
  • Refer immediately to hospital if:
    • Patient is unconscious or having convulsions
    • Severe breathing difficulty or blue lips
    • Severe dehydration (sunken eyes, no urine, lethargy)
    • Severe bleeding

COVID-19 Home Care Guidelines

  • Isolate the patient in a well-ventilated room.
  • Monitor temperature and oxygen saturation (if pulse oximeter is available).
  • Encourage fluids and a nutritious diet.
  • Use masks and hand hygiene for patient and caregiver.
  • Seek urgent care if:
    • Oxygen saturation falls below 94%
    • Breathlessness increases
    • Chest pain, confusion, or bluish lips develop

General Medical Best Practices

  2. Use masks and gloves when dealing with wounds or suspected infections.
  3. Maintain patient privacy and dignity at all times.
