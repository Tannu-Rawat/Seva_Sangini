Medical Guidelines

Antenatal Care for Pregnant Women

  • Register pregnancy early (preferably before 12 weeks) at nearest health center.
  • Minimum 4 antenatal check-ups: at booking, 2nd trimester, 3rd trimester, and before delivery.
  • Check blood pressure, hemoglobin, urine, and screen for HIV, syphilis, hepatitis B.
  • Provide iron-folic acid and calcium tablets as per government protocol.
  • Tetanus-diphtheria (Td) immunization as per schedule.
  • Screen for high-risk factors: bleeding, swelling, high BP, severe anemia, fever.
  • Advise rest, nutrition, and danger sign awareness (bleeding, convulsions, severe pain, breathlessness).
  • Refer immediately if any danger sign is present.

Safe Delivery and Postnatal Care

  • Promote institutional delivery (government hospital/PHC).
  • Ensure clean hands and sterile instruments during delivery.
  • Monitor for postpartum bleeding, fever, or convulsions.
  • Early breastfeeding within 1 hour of birth; exclusive breastfeeding for 6 months.
  • Postnatal visits: at least 3 (within 48 hours, on day 7, and day 42).
  • Check for maternal infection, wound healing, and mental wellbeing.
  • Refer to higher center if heavy bleeding, fever, severe pain, or baby not feeding.

Newborn Care (0–28 days)

  • Keep baby warm (skin-to-skin/kangaroo care), delay bathing for 24 hours.
  • Initiate breastfeeding within 1 hour; no pre-lacteal feeds.
  • Give birth dose vaccines (BCG, OPV, Hepatitis B) as per schedule.
  • Daily cord care: keep cord stump clean and dry, do not apply anything.
  • Recognize danger signs: poor feeding, fast breathing, chest indrawing, fever, hypothermia, convulsions, yellow palms/soles.
  • Refer urgently if any danger sign present.
  • Home visits by ASHA: on day 1, 3, 7, 14, 21, 28 for weight, feeding, and illness check.

Child Health Guidelines (up to 5 years)

  • Ensure full immunization as per National Immunization Schedule (BCG, OPV, Pentavalent, Measles-Rubella, etc.).
  • Monitor growth: weigh monthly for infants, quarterly for toddlers.
  • Vitamin A supplementation as per schedule.
  • Promote exclusive breastfeeding till 6 months; introduce complementary feeding at 6 months.
  • Advise on handwashing, safe water, and sanitation to prevent diarrhea and infections.
  • Recognize danger signs: persistent vomiting, convulsions, lethargy, inability to feed, severe dehydration, fast breathing.
  • Refer to health center if danger signs present.

Management of Common Childhood Illnesses

  • For diarrhea: Give Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS) and zinc tablets; continue feeding/breastfeeding.
  • For cough/cold: Keep child warm, ensure fluids, seek care if fast breathing or chest indrawing.
  • For fever: Tepid sponging, paracetamol if needed, seek care if fever persists >3 days or child is lethargic.
  • For malnutrition: Refer to Anganwadi/PHC for growth monitoring and supplementary nutrition.

COVID-19 Home Care Guidelines

  • Isolate the patient in a well-ventilated room.
  • Monitor temperature and oxygen saturation (if pulse oximeter is available).
  • Encourage fluids and a nutritious diet.
  • Use masks and hand hygiene for patient and caregiver.
  • Seek urgent care if:
    • Oxygen saturation falls below 94%
    • Breathlessness increases
    • Chest pain, confusion, or bluish lips develop

General Medical Best Practices

  1. Always wash hands before and after patient contact.
  2. Use masks and gloves when dealing with wounds or suspected infections.
  3. Maintain patient privacy and dignity at all times.
  4. Document all findings and care provided in the patient’s record.
  5. Refer to higher centers if unsure or if patient does not improve.
