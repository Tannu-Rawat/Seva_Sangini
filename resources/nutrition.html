Nutrition Guidelines for Pregnant Women

  • Recommended Intake: 2,250 kcal/day and 78g protein/day (sedentary worker); 2,500 kcal and 74g protein/day for lactating mothers (0–6 months)
    Source: ICMR, National Health Mission
  • Supplementary Nutrition: Under ICDS/Anganwadi, pregnant and lactating women receive Take Home Ration (THR) or hot cooked meals for at least 300 days/year, providing 600 kcal and 18–20g protein/day[9][10][12].
  • Iron and Folic Acid: Daily iron-folic acid tablets to prevent anemia. Eat iron-rich foods (spinach, lentils, jaggery) and vitamin C-rich foods (citrus) to enhance absorption.
  • Calcium: Daily calcium supplements. Include milk, curd, ragi, sesame seeds, and small fish with bones in the diet.
  • Meal Hygiene: Ensure food safety and hygiene during preparation and serving, especially in facilities[3].
  • Diet Diversity: Eat a variety of foods: cereals, pulses, vegetables, fruits, and moderate animal foods. Avoid excess salt, sugar, and processed foods[11][14].
  • Exclusive Breastfeeding: Promote for the first 6 months post-delivery[12].

Child Nutrition Guidelines (0–5 years)

  • 0–6 months: Exclusive breastfeeding, no water or other foods[12].
  • 6–24 months: Continue breastfeeding; introduce complementary foods (mashed dal, rice, khichdi, fruits, vegetables). Feed 2–3 times/day at 6–8 months, increasing to 3–4 times plus snacks by 12 months[6].
  • 1–2 years: Family foods, frequent small meals, milk, eggs, fruits, vegetables, grains. Example diet: idli/dosa with vegetable curry, fruit, rice with dal, snacks like upma/bread, milk at bedtime[6].
  • 3–5 years: Balanced diet with all food groups; hot cooked meals and snacks provided at Anganwadi centers for 3–6 years. Severely malnourished children receive additional supplements[5][9].
  • Supplementary Nutrition: Children 6–36 months: THR with 500 kcal and 12–15g protein/day. Severely malnourished: 800 kcal and 20–25g protein/day. Children 3–6 years: hot cooked meal plus snack at Anganwadi[5][9].
  • Local Foods: Use affordable, locally available foods: amaranth, sattu, ragi, garden cress seeds, small fish, sprouted grains[6][2].
  • Meal Hygiene: Ensure handwashing and safe preparation to prevent infections[11].

Supplementary Nutrition & Anganwadi Services

  • Entitlement: Every pregnant woman, lactating mother, and child (6 months–6 years) is entitled to free meals or THR at Anganwadi centers for at least 300 days/year, as per the National Food Security Act[12][8].
  • Distribution: Fixed days for mixing and distribution; monitored by community committees to ensure quality and coverage[8].
  • Nutrition Monitoring: Growth monitoring, counseling, and home visits are part of the service package.
  • Special Days: Village Health and Nutrition Days (VHNDs) are held monthly to promote awareness and monitor nutrition[4].

ICMR 2024 Dietary Guidelines: Key Points

  • Eat a variety of foods for a balanced diet: cereals, pulses, vegetables, fruits, nuts, and moderate animal foods[14].
  • For a 2000 kcal diet: 250g cereals, 400g vegetables, 100g fruits, 85g pulses/eggs/flesh foods, 35g nuts/seeds, 27g fat/oils per day[14].
  • Restrict salt, sugar, and processed foods. Use minimal oil/fats and salt for taste.
  • Drink plenty of water and practice regular physical activity.
  • Promote exclusive breastfeeding for 6 months, continue as long as possible[11][14].
  • Adopt safe cooking and food handling practices.

Practical Tips for Field Workers

  • Encourage mothers to register pregnancies early and attend all check-ups.
  • Promote use of Anganwadi and health center services for nutrition and growth monitoring.
  • Demonstrate handwashing and safe food preparation during home visits and VHNDs.
  • Advise on local, affordable food alternatives to improve diet diversity.
  • Monitor for signs of malnutrition and refer severe cases promptly.
